These are the faces you'll meet at Ranjani Groth Studios

RANJANI

Wanderlust is imprinted on my heart. I’ve lived in more than a dozen places across the globe, yet wherever I call home, my camera is always at my side. As a teen, I’d drag a chair into the Illinois woods and convince my sisters to wear prom dresses for photo shoots with our mom’s old film camera. I found connection with my hosts in Tajikistan, though we didn’t share a language, through the lens of my camera. In college, I studied photojournalism and worked for more than a decade as a wedding photographer. All those couples taught me how to be an expert wrangler, problem solver (I always carry a mini-sewing kit now) and mood lightener. I pivoted to studio portraits five years ago, and haven’t looked back. Today, I l o v e the happy chaos of everyone (plus the dog!) piling in for a group photo. The interaction there—between grandmas and grandkids, moms and sons—is just brimming with life.

Here's what studio means today


Myth - You need to dress formally
Reality - Absolutely not! You feel your best when you’re in your favorites, so wear ‘em! That can be as formal (or informal!) as you’d like. In fact, we’ve had families who let their kids come in costume, and it was a ton of fun for everyone.


Myth - You’re a number on a calendar
Reality - Not here you’re not. Every single member of our studio is here to help you feel welcome, comfortable, and cared for. You are our priority from the moment we connect.


Myth - Studio lighting is harsh
Reality - We have years of studio experience and technical expertise to use lighting to our advantage. Take a look at our galleries to see the way we work with light to create warm images with depth and emotion.


Myth - Stiff posing and frozen smiles, eek!
Reality - Of course we always make sure to capture at least one image of everyone smiling at the camera. But we’ve found that your favorites are the ones where you’re simply interacting with your people—comfortable, natural + authentic.
